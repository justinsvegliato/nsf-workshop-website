I'm a second year grad student at UMass Amherst working in the Resource-Bounded Reasoning Lab under Dr. Shlomo Zilberstein. My research focuses mainly on problems in automated reasoning. I currently work on metareasoning techniques that monitor and control algorithms that trade off decision quality with computation time. I also work on planning algorithms that enable semi-autonomous systems to keep humans in the loop by smoothly transferring control to them during difficult tasks. Before coming to grad school, I worked as a software developer on Wall Street. Outside of all of that, I'm also interested in many big problems in philosophy, especially free will, mind, metaphysics, and epistemology. Feel free to reach out if you want to chat!
